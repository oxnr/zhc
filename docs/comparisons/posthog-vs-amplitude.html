PostHog vs Amplitude: Comprehensive Comparison 2026

PostHog and Amplitude represent two fundamentally different philosophies in product analytics. PostHog is open-source, self-hostable, and bundles analytics with session replay, feature flags, and A/B testing into a single platform. Amplitude is a mature, enterprise-grade analytics product known for its deep behavioral analysis, powerful cohort tools, and polished collaboration features. This comparison covers everything you need to pick the right tool for your product team in 2026.

PostHog Overview

PostHog was founded in 2020 and has grown rapidly as the leading open-source product analytics platform. The company raised $27M in Series B funding and operates with radical transparency — their entire codebase, handbook, and strategy are public on GitHub. PostHog's core differentiator is breadth: it combines product analytics, session recording, feature flags, A/B testing, surveys, and a data warehouse into a single platform. You can self-host on your own infrastructure for complete data control, or use their managed cloud. PostHog uses a warehouse-native architecture backed by ClickHouse, letting teams query raw event data with SQL. The platform is especially popular with developer-centric teams who value open-source ethos, data ownership, and the ability to extend the platform through apps and plugins.

Amplitude Overview

Amplitude was founded in 2012 and went public in 2021 (NASDAQ: AMPL). It serves over 2,600 customers including Walmart, NBC Universal, Atlassian, and PayPal, making it one of the most widely adopted enterprise analytics platforms. Amplitude's strength lies in the depth of its behavioral analytics: advanced funnel analysis, sophisticated cohort builders, user journey mapping, and predictive analytics powered by machine learning. The platform has expanded into experimentation (Amplitude Experiment), session replay, and a customer data platform (Amplitude CDP). Amplitude excels at helping product and growth teams answer complex behavioral questions without writing SQL, through an intuitive chart builder and collaborative notebooks. Its governance features, role-based access, and compliance certifications (SOC 2 Type II, GDPR, HIPAA) make it a natural fit for regulated industries and large organizations.

Pricing Comparison

PostHog uses usage-based pricing with a generous free tier:

  • Free: 1M events/mo, 5K session recordings, 1M feature flag requests, 250 survey responses
  • Product analytics: $0.00031/event after 1M free (volume discounts apply)
  • Session replay: $0.005/recording after 5K free
  • Feature flags: $0.0001/request after 1M free
  • Surveys: $0.20/response after 250 free
  • No seat-based pricing — unlimited users on all plans

Amplitude pricing:

  • Starter (Free): Up to 50K monthly tracked users (MTUs), core analytics charts, unlimited seats
  • Plus: From $49/mo — advanced analytics, unlimited saved charts, root cause analysis
  • Growth: Custom pricing — behavioral cohorts, causal analysis, data governance, SSO
  • Enterprise: Custom pricing — predictions, custom roles, dedicated CSM, HIPAA BAA, 99.9% SLA
  • Amplitude Experiment and CDP are priced separately on top of Analytics

Key pricing insight: PostHog's usage-based model is significantly cheaper for most startups and mid-market companies — a team tracking 5M events/month pays roughly $125/month with no per-seat fees. Amplitude's free tier is generous on MTUs (50K), but the jump to Growth or Enterprise for governance, cohorts, and SSO is a large, opaque cost increase. Where Amplitude wins on pricing is for very large organizations that negotiate enterprise deals with volume commitments — the per-event cost can be competitive, and you get white-glove support. PostHog is the clear cost winner for teams under ~100M events/month who want all-in-one tooling without sales calls.

PostHog Pros and Cons

Pros

  • + All-in-one platform: analytics, session replay, feature flags, A/B tests, and surveys
  • + Open-source with full self-hosting — total control over your data
  • + Generous free tier (1M events/mo) and transparent usage-based pricing
  • + No per-seat pricing — unlimited users regardless of plan
  • + Direct SQL access to raw data (HogQL) for maximum flexibility

Cons

  • - Analytics depth is shallower than Amplitude — no predictive analytics, weaker cohort tools
  • - Self-hosting requires DevOps capacity (ClickHouse, Kafka, PostgreSQL)
  • - Data governance and taxonomy management are still maturing
  • - Less polished UI and collaboration features compared to Amplitude
  • - Enterprise compliance certifications (HIPAA, SOC 2) are newer and less battle-tested

Amplitude Pros and Cons

Pros

  • + Deepest behavioral analytics in the market — cohorts, journeys, and predictive models
  • + Polished, intuitive chart builder that non-technical users can master quickly
  • + Mature data governance with event blocking, property enforcement, and approval flows
  • + Enterprise-grade compliance — SOC 2 Type II, GDPR, HIPAA BAA with audit trails
  • + Strong collaboration: notebooks, team spaces, and annotation features

Cons

  • - Opaque enterprise pricing — requires a sales call for Growth and Enterprise tiers
  • - No self-hosting option — your data lives on Amplitude's infrastructure
  • - Session replay, experimentation, and CDP are separate paid products
  • - Can become expensive quickly once you exceed the free MTU threshold
  • - Feature flags require Experiment add-on; no standalone flag management

Which Should You Choose?

This decision often comes down to team DNA. Engineering-led product teams tend to gravitate toward PostHog. Product and growth teams in larger organizations lean toward Amplitude. Both are excellent — the question is which strengths matter more for your context.

Choose PostHog if...

  • You want analytics, session replay, feature flags, and A/B testing in one tool
  • Data ownership and self-hosting are requirements (GDPR, sensitive data)
  • You're an engineering-led team comfortable with SQL and open-source tooling
  • You want transparent, usage-based pricing without sales negotiations
  • You're a startup or mid-market company tracking under 100M events/month

Choose Amplitude if...

  • You need the deepest behavioral analytics — predictive models, advanced cohorts, journey maps
  • Your team is mostly PMs and analysts who prefer no-code chart builders
  • Data governance, compliance (HIPAA, SOC 2), and audit trails are non-negotiable
  • You're an enterprise team willing to invest in a premium, polished analytics platform
  • Collaboration features like notebooks, annotations, and team spaces matter to you
